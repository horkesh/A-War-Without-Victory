# ARBiH (Armija Republike Bosne i Hercegovine) - Master Order of Battle
## Army of the Republic of Bosnia and Herzegovina 1992-1995

**Source Authority:** Balkan Battlegrounds Vol I & II, CIA Analysis

---

## SUPREME COMMAND STRUCTURE

**Political Leadership:**
- President / Commander-in-Chief: Alija Izetbegović
- Presidency of Bosnia and Herzegovina (collective leadership)

**Military Command Evolution:**

**Phase 1 (April-December 1992): Formation**
- Chief of Staff: Sefer Halilović (April-June 1993)
- Deputy Chief: Rasim Delić
- Status: Building from scratch, former TO and JNA Muslim officers

**Phase 2 (June 1993-November 1993): Reorganization**
- Commander: Sefer Halilović (short period)
- Chief of General Staff: Rasim Delić

**Phase 3 (November 1993-1995): Mature Structure**
- Commander of Main Staff: General Rasim Delić (November 1993-1995)
- Chief of General Staff: Enver Hadžihasanović (later)

**HQ:** Sarajevo (various locations due to siege)

---

## CORPS STRUCTURE OVERVIEW

ARBiH organized into **7 corps** (formed gradually 1992-1993):

1. **1st Corps** (Sarajevo) - Largest, defending capital
2. **2nd Corps** (Tuzla) - Industrial base, northeastern BiH
3. **3rd Corps** (Zenica) - Industrial base, central Bosnia
4. **4th Corps** (Mostar) - Formed late, Herzegovina
5. **5th Corps** (Bihać) - Isolated pocket, internal division (Abdić)
6. **6th Corps** (Konjic) - Formed late, central Herzegovina
7. **7th Corps** (Travnik) - Central Bosnia, formed 1993

**Plus isolated enclaves:**
- Srebrenica, Goražde, Žepa (Drina Valley) - Under siege, separate commands

**Total Strength Evolution:**
- April 1992: ~60,000-80,000 (many unarmed or lightly armed)
- December 1992: ~110,000-130,000
- 1993: ~135,000-155,000
- 1994: ~165,000-180,000
- 1995: ~180,000-200,000

## 1ST CORPS (SARAJEVO)

**HQ:** Sarajevo
**Commander:** Brigadier General Vahid Karavelić (1992-1995)
**Strength:** 20,000 (1992) → 50,000-55,000 (1995)

**Area:** Sarajevo city and immediate surroundings
**Mission:** Defense of besieged capital

### Key Details:
- Largest ARBiH corps
- Under continuous siege 1,425 days (May 1992-Feb 1996)
- Sarajevo Tunnel (1993-1995): Critical supply lifeline under airport
- ~10,000-12,000 civilian deaths during siege
- Limited offensive capability due to encirclement

**Equipment:** Small arms, mortars (82mm, 60mm), minimal artillery

**Strategic Importance:** Defending capital, tying down VRS Sarajevo-Romanija Corps

---

## 2ND CORPS (TUZLA)

**HQ:** Tuzla
**Commanders:** Brigadier General Sead Delić, later Hazim Šadić
**Strength:** 10,000-15,000 (1992) → 45,000-50,000 (1995)

**Area:** Northeastern BiH (Tuzla, Gradačac, Kladanj, **Sapna**)
**Mission:** Defense of industrial base, pressure on Posavina Corridor

### Key Operational Groups:

**1st OG (Later 21st Division):**
- 107th "Gradačac" Brigade (HVO elements, cooperative)
- 108th "Brčko" Brigade (HVO elements, cooperative)

**3rd OG (Sapna-Kalesija Sector):**
- **Sapna stronghold brigades (CRITICAL)**
- Forward positions blocking VRS expansion

**5th OG (Later 25th Division):**
- 115th "Zrinski" Brigade (HVO, operated 1992-Jan 1994, then disbanded)
- Tuzla defense units

**Area Control:**
- **Tuzla:** Industrial stronghold (~100,000 population)
- **Gradačac, Živinice, Kalesija, Kladanj**
- **SAPNA MUNICIPALITY:** Critical stronghold, forward position
- **Teočak:** Forward position
- Maglaj: Besieged pocket (ARBiH holding)

**Assessment:** Most offensive-capable ARBiH corps, best HVO cooperation

**Equipment:** Better than most corps - captured weapons, some production

---

## 3RD CORPS (ZENICA)

**HQ:** Zenica
**Commander:** Enver Hadžihasanović (1992-1994), later Sakib Mahmuljin
**Strength:** 15,000-20,000 (1992) → 50,000-55,000 (1995)

**Area:** Central Bosnia (Zenica, Travnik, Bugojno, Kakanj)
**Mission:** Defense of industrial base, two-front war (VRS and HVO 1993)

### Elite Units:

**7th Muslim Brigade:**
- Elite shock troops, ~1,500-2,000
- Most effective ARBiH infantry
- Some mujahideen, controversial

**17th Krajina Brigade**
**306th Bugojno Brigade** (after capture from HVO July 1993)
**307th Brigade**

**Area Control:**
- **Zenica:** Industrial center, steel works, ammunition production
- **Travnik:** Captured from HVO 1993
- **Kakanj, Visoko, Breza:** Industrial/production
- **Bugojno:** Captured from HVO July 1993
- **Fojnica:** Captured from HVO

**Lost:** Jajce (to VRS Oct 29, 1992), Vareš (to VRS Nov 1993)

**HVO War (1993):**
- Brutal fighting in central Bosnia
- Besieged HVO Vitez-Busovača enclave
- Ahmići Massacre (HVO, April 16, 1993)
- Stupni Do Massacre (ARBiH, Oct 23, 1993)

**Strategic Importance:** Industrial production (Zenica), central position

---

## 4TH CORPS (MOSTAR)

**HQ:** Mostar (east bank)
**Strength:** Formed 1993, ~8,000-12,000 (1993-1994) → 12,000-15,000 (1995)

**Area:** Mostar east bank
**Mission:** Defense during HVO siege, integration after Washington Agreement

**Siege of East Mostar (May 1993-Feb 1994):**
- Population: ~55,000 (including refugees)
- HVO complete siege
- Starvation, daily shelling
- Stari Most destroyed by HVO (Nov 9, 1993)
- ~2,000 civilian deaths

**Equipment:** Minimal during siege, improved after Federation

---

## 5TH CORPS (BIHAĆ)

**HQ:** Bihać
**Commanders:** Ramiz Drekovic (1992-1993), Atif Dudaković (1993-1995)
**Strength:** 10,000-15,000 (1992) → 25,000-30,000 (1995, after Abdić defeated)

**Area:** Bihać pocket (completely isolated enclave)
**Mission:** Defense of pocket, internal war with Abdić

### Internal Division:

**Mainstream 5th Corps (Loyal to Sarajevo):**
- Commander: Atif Dudaković
- Strength: ~18,000-20,000
- Base: Bihać city, Cazin

**Fikret Abdić's "Autonomous Province":**
- Renegade force (1993-1995)
- Strength: ~7,000-10,000
- Base: Velika Kladuša
- Cooperated with VRS/RSK against 5th Corps
- Defeated 1995

**Brigades:** 501st Bihać, 502nd Cazin, 505th Buzim, 511th Bužim

**Strategic Situation:**
- Completely isolated (1992-1995)
- Surrounded by VRS 2nd Krajina Corps + RSK
- Population: ~180,000 in pocket
- 1994 crisis: Nearly fell (VRS+RSK+Abdić offensive)
- 1995: Relieved by Federation offensives

**Assessment:** Remarkable resilience despite total isolation and internal war

---

## 6TH CORPS (KONJIC)

**HQ:** Konjic
**Strength:** Formed late 1992/early 1993, ~8,000-12,000 (1993-1994) → 12,000-15,000 (1995)

**Area:** Central Herzegovina (Konjic, Jablanica)
**Mission:** Defense of Sarajevo supply route (Mt. Igman)

**Area Control:**
- **Konjic:** Critical for Sarajevo supply, Mt. Igman route
- **Jablanica:** Strategic position
- Sarajevo Tunnel entrance area

**Strategic Importance:** Only land route to besieged Sarajevo

---

## 7TH CORPS (TRAVNIK)

**HQ:** Travnik
**Strength:** Formed 1993, ~15,000-18,000 (1993-1994) → 20,000-25,000 (1995)

**Area:** Central Bosnia (Travnik region)
**Mission:** Operations against VRS, central Bosnia

**Key Unit:** 370th "Donji Vakuf" Mountain Brigade (elite mountain unit)

**Major Operations:**
- Mt. Vlašić Breakthrough (April 1995): Broke through VRS 1st Krajina Corps
- 1995 Federation Offensives: Coordinated operations with 3rd Corps, HV

## DRINA VALLEY ENCLAVES

### SREBRENICA POCKET

**Commander:** Naser Orić
**Strength:** 1,000-2,000 (1992) → 3,500-4,000 (1993-1995)
**Population:** 9,000 pre-war → 40,000-45,000 (with refugees)

**Timeline:**
- 1992: Local defense, raids for supplies
- March 1993: Crisis, ~2-3 rounds/rifle, near collapse
- April 1993: UN Safe Area declared (saved pocket)
- July 11, 1995: VRS captured, ~8,000 killed (genocide)

**UNPROFOR:** Dutch battalion ~400 (failed to protect)

**Equipment:** Small arms, hunting rifles, chronic ammunition shortage

---

### GORAŽDE POCKET

**Strength:** 2,000-3,000 (1992) → 4,000-6,000 (1995)
**Population:** 30,000 pre-war → 60,000-65,000 (with refugees)

**Timeline:**
- 1992-1995: Continuous siege
- April 1994: VRS offensive, NATO airstrikes saved pocket
- 1995: Held until Dayton

**Assessment:** Better organized, better supplied than Srebrenica, survived war

---

### ŽEPA POCKET

**Commander:** Avdo Palić (Major)
**Strength:** 800-1,200
**Population:** 5,000 pre-war → 10,000-15,000

**Timeline:**
- 1992-1995: Continuous siege
- July 1995: VRS captured after Srebrenica fell
- Avdo Palić captured, killed (war crime)

**Assessment:** Smallest, most isolated pocket

## ARBIH EQUIPMENT

**Small Arms:**
- Zastava M70 (AK variant)
- Captured JNA/VRS weapons
- Hunting rifles, improvised weapons

**Heavy Weapons (Severely Limited):**
- Artillery: Minimal, mostly captured (105mm, 122mm)
- Mortars: 82mm, 120mm, 60mm (primary indirect fire)
- Tanks: ~30-50 total all corps (captured T-55)
- No effective air defense

**Local Production:**
- Zenica: Ammunition from steel works
- Breza: Munitions factory
- Improvised mortars, explosives

**Arms Embargo:** Critical constraint, prevented legal arms purchases

## ARBIH STRATEGIC ASSESSMENT

**Strengths:**
- Large manpower base (~44% Bosniak population)
- High morale (defending homes)
- Effective urban warfare
- Resilience despite overwhelming odds

**Weaknesses:**
- Severe shortage heavy weapons
- Chronic ammunition shortages
- Arms embargo prevented resupply
- Fragmented territory (isolated pockets)
- Initially no training, no organization

**Evolution:**
- 1992: Desperate defense, volunteers
- 1993: Professionalization, two-front war (VRS + HVO)
- 1994: Federation alliance transformed strategic situation
- 1995: Coordinated offensives with HV/HVO, territorial gains

**Territorial Control:**
- April 1992: ~10-15%
- December 1992: ~20-25% (fragmented)
- 1993: ~20-25%
- 1994: ~25-30% (Federation)
- October 1995: ~51% (Dayton Agreement)
